1. 12-Bar Blues Structure

The classic I–I–I–I–IV–IV–I–I–V–IV–I–I progression, each bar = 4 beats.

Bars 1–4: | I | I | I | I |
Bars 5–6: | IV | IV |
Bars 7–8: | I | I |
Bars 9–10:| V | IV |
Bars 11–12:| I | I |

3. Shuffle Rhythm

Triplet feel: play swing eighths: long–short pattern.

// Swing eighths count: 1 - a 2 - a 3 - a 4 - a
| D   ~   U   ~   D   ~   U   ~ |  
// where ~ ties first triplet pair

4. Turnaround Patterns

Classic last-bar turnaround in E to loop back.

// Simple E7 turnaround
e|-------------0----0-----|
B|-----------0----0-------|
G|---------1----1---------|
D|--2-0-2-4----2-----2----|
A|------------------------|
E|------------------------|

// A7→E7→B7→A7 in key of E
| A7 | E7 | B7 | A7 |

6. Minor Blues Progression

Minor variant uses i–iv–i–i–iv–iv–i–i–v–iv–i–v.

// In A minor
| Am | Am | Am | Am |
| Dm | Dm | Am | Am |
| Em | Dm | Am | Em |
