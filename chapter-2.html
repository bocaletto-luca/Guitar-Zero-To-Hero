Chapter 2 — Technical Foundations

2.1 Posture & Grip

Correct posture and grip set the foundation for healthy, efficient technique. Maintain relaxed shoulders, a straight back, and neutral wrists.

Seated posture
  • Sit upright, feet flat on floor
  • Guitar body rests on right thigh (righthanded)
  • Neck angled ~15° upward
  • Fretting wrist neutral, not bent
Standing posture
  • Use strap at comfortable height
  • Guitar angle similar to sitting
  • Balance weight evenly
  • Wrist alignment straight
Grip the neck

Thumb rests mid‐neck behind the fretboard. Thumb height should align with the 2nd finger joint for leverage.

2.2 Right-Hand Techniques

Explore three core approaches: strumming with a pick, fingerstyle, and palm muting for tonal variation.

Strumming basics
  • Downstroke (D) & upstroke (U)
  • Relaxed wrist hinge
  • Consistent motion, minimal elbow
  • Start on downbeats
// Eighth-note pattern
| D   U   D   U |
  1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &
Fingerstyle
  • p (thumb) → E/A/D strings
  • i (index) → G string
  • m (middle) → B string
  • a (ring) → e string
// Simple arpeggio
p     i     m     a
E|------0-------|
B|----1---1-----|
G|--0-------0---|
D|2-----------2-|
A|3-------------|
E|--------------|
Palm mute
  • Rest side of palm on bridge
  • Light pressure for muted “chuck”
  • Combine with strumming patterns
// Muted quarter notes
| PM– D – PM– D |

2.3 Left-Hand Techniques

Develop clear, buzz-free notes by placing your fingertip just behind the fret wire and applying even pressure.

Finger pressure & placement
  • Press with fingertip, not pad
  • Place close to fret wire (<3 mm)
  • Apply enough force to ring cleanly
Warm-up chromatic exercise 
e|0-1-2-3-2-1-0|  
B|0-1-2-3-2-1-0|

// Repeat slowly, then increase tempo
Stretching

Open hand and finger stretches before playing reduce tension and prevent injury.

2.5 Rhythm & Timing

Solid timing is built with a metronome and by reading simple rhythmic notation.

Note values
  • Quarter note (♩) = 1 beat
  • Eighth note (♪) = 1/2 beat
  • Sixteenth note (♬) = 1/4 beat
Metronome drill
  • Set → 60 bpm
  • Play quarter notes: 1 2 3 4
  • Play eighths: 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &
  • Combine with chord changes

Example pattern

// Em → G with eighths at ♩=60
| Em ♪♪♪♪ | G ♪♪♪♪ |  
// 1&2&3&4& | 1&2&3&4&
