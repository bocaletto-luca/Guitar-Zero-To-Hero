3.1 Reading Tablature

Tablature (TAB) displays strings and fret numbers, guiding your fingers directly. It's the most intuitive way to learn guitar pieces quickly.

Strings order

Top line = 1st string (high e), bottom line = 6th string (low E).

Vertical alignment shows simultaneous notes (chords or double-stops). Brackets, symbols, and spacing communicate techniques and rhythm.

3.2 Staff Notation Basics

The staff uses five lines and four spaces to represent pitches. Each line/space corresponds to a note name; ledger lines extend the range.

Clef & staff

3.4 Intervals & Harmony

Intervals measure distance between two pitches. Recognize intervals to build chords, melodies, and progressions.

Common intervals
  • Unison (P1)
  • Minor 2nd (m2)
  • Major 2nd (M2)
  • Minor 3rd (m3)
  • Major 3rd (M3)
  • Perfect 4th (P4)
  • Perfect 5th (P5)
  • Octave (P8)
Building triads

Major triad = root + M3 + P5
Minor triad = root + m3 + P5
