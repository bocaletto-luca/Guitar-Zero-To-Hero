Chapter 5 — Advanced Techniques

5.1 Open & Creative Tunings

Alternate tunings unlock new voicings and resonances. Experiment with these common setups.

TuningStrings (6→1)Characteristic
Open D D A D F♯ A D Full D major chord on open strings
Open C C G C G C E Rich C major resonance, deep bass
DADGAD D A D G A D Modal, droning possibilities
Exploration

Strum open strings, then move single fingers to discover new chord shapes.

5.2 Percussive Techniques

Add rhythmic elements by tapping and slapping different parts of the guitar body.

Body slap

With the fleshy palm, strike the lower bout near the bridge for a kick-drum sound.

Thumb slap

Use thumb side to hit the lower strings against the fretboard for a snare-like pop.

Tap rhythm 
// Example pattern:
| Tap – – Tap | Tap Tap – – |

5.3 Tapping & Harmonics

G|--------------------------|

5.4 Solo Arrangement

Combine melody, harmony, and bass in one performance for a complete solo piece.

// C chord melody & bass
e|--0-----------0------------|
B|----1---0h1-----1-0--------|
G|------0---------0---0------|
D|2-----------2--------2-----|
A|3--------------------------|
E|---------------------------|
Approach

Outline root movement in bass, then weave melody on higher strings.

5.5 Improvisation

Use scales, motifs, and call-and-response to craft solos that speak.

Scale foundation

Start with minor pentatonic box 1, then add notes of the major scale for color.

Motif development

Create a 2-note phrase, repeat with variation in rhythm or pitch.

Call & response

Phrase (“call”), then answer with a contrasting lick (“response”).

// Example call & response in E minor
Call:    e|--0-3-0--------|
         B|--------3-0----|
Response:e|--5-7-5--------|
         B|--------7-5----|
