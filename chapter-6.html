Chapter 6 — Repertoire & Performance

6.1 Building Repertoire

A well-rounded repertoire includes styles, tempos, and technical challenges. Curate songs that reinforce your strengths and push new skills.

Genre variety
  • Acoustic folk ballads
  • Pop strumming anthems
  • Blues riffs & licks
  • Fingerstyle instrumentals
Difficulty balance
  • Easy: open chords, simple rhythms
  • Medium: barre chords, basic arpeggios
  • Advanced: fingerstyle arrangements, solo embellishments
Learning process
  1. Listen to reference recording
  2. Learn chord progression
  3. Add melody or riff
  4. Refine transitions & timing
  5. Practice with metronome

6.2 Playing with Others

Collaboration demands active listening, dynamic control, and space. Learn how to integrate your guitar in different band settings.

Rhythm section
  • Lock in with drums & bass
  • Use consistent strumming patterns
  • Adjust volume to support vocals
Lead & fill
  • Complement melody with tasteful licks
  • Leave sonic space: don’t overcrowd arrangement
  • Use call-and-response with other instruments
Communication

Discuss song arrangement and cues before playing. Establish eye contact or simple signals for transitions.

6.3 Recording & Amplification

Capture your best tone with minimal gear: microphone placement, DI recording, and simple effects.

Microphone techniques
  • Condenser at 12th fret, 20–30 cm back
  • Dynamic near bridge for warmth
  • Blend signals for depth
Direct input (DI)
  • Use audio interface with DI input
  • Apply amp-simulation plugin
  • Record clean + effected track separately
Quick home setup
  1. Interface → computer
  2. Guitar → interface (DI) or mic → interface
  3. DAW settings: 44.1 kHz, 24-bit
  4. Light compression & EQ for clarity

6.4 Stage Presence & Mindset

Confidence on stage stems from preparation and connection with the audience. Cultivate a performance persona that feels authentic.

Preparation
  • Memorize structures & key changes
  • Run-through in performance attire
  • Warm up physically and mentally
Engagement
  • Maintain eye contact
  • Use body language & movement
  • Speak or introduce songs between pieces
Managing nerves

Use deep breathing and visualization. Focus on sharing music rather than personal evaluation.
