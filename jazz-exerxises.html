1. Introduction

Jazz guitar combines rich harmonies, syncopated rhythms and melodic improvisation. Mastering chord voicings and ii–V–I is essential for comping and soloing.

2. Extended Chord Voicings

Common jazz voicings use 7th, 9th and 13th chords. Focus on 4‐string shapes:

// Cmaj7 (root 6th string)
e|--x--
B|--5--  (9)
G|--4--  (7)
D|--5--  (3)
A|--3--  (1)
E|--x--

// Dm9 (root 5th string)
e|--5--  (9)
B|--5--  (7)
G|--5--  (3)
D|--7--  (1)
A|--5--  (x)
E|--x--

// G13 (root 6th string)
e|--5--  (13)
B|--5--  (7)
G|--5--  (3)
D|--4--  (13)
A|--5--  (1)
E|--3--  (x)

3. Shell Voicings

3-note “shell” chords omit extensions, leaving root, 3rd and 7th. Great for comping.

// Shell voicings (root on 6th string)
Cmaj7: e|--x--  B|--x--  G|--5--  D|--5--  A|--3--  E|--x--
Dm7  : e|--x--  B|--6--  G|--5--  D|--7--  A|--5--  E|--x--
G7   : e|--x--  B|--3--  G|--4--  D|--3--  A|--x--  E|--3--

4. ii–V–I Progressions

Foundation of jazz harmony. Practice in C major and minor.

// Major ii–V–I in C

D|--7----|--4----|--5---|--5---|
A|--x----|--5----|--3---|--3---|

5. Comping Rhythms

Syncopated “Freddie Green” style with light touch on chords.

// Simple swing comp (2 & 4 hits)
| — x — x — x — x |
| 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & |

// Bossa nova comping
| B   x   C   x   |  
| 1 e & a 2 e & a |  
// B=beat chord, C=chord on ‘&’ of 2
